Tiger

There are approximately 5,578 tigers left in the wild.

The Bengal tiger is the most numerous subspecies, accounting for about half of the total tiger population

Each tiger has a unique stripe pattern, similar to a human fingerprint, which allows researchers to identify individuals in the wild using camera traps.

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is a large cat and a member of the genus Panthera native to Asia

It is the largest living cat species, with the Siberian (Amur) tiger being the largest, measuring up to 4 meters (13 feet) in total length and weighing up to 300 kg (660 pounds).

Despite their strength and ferocity, tigers are endangered, with their range having declined by 53% to 68% since the late 1990s and losing at least 93% of their historic range since the early 20th century.
